# Character Spec: Quinn Almeida
**Role**: The Pragmatic Accountant
**Animal**: Hedgehog
**Career**: Finance Specialist
**Tier**: Secondary (LinkedIn 2026 Expansion)

---

## 1. Core Profile
*   **One-Line High Concept**: Former loan officer turned financial literacy advocate who discovered that teaching wealth-building matters more than accumulating it
*   **The "Want" (Desire)**: To help people understand that numbers are tools for freedom, not chains
*   **The "Ghost" (Trauma)**: Guilt from years of approving predatory loans that trapped people in debt cycles
*   **The Lie They Believe**: That being good with money means you must be cold with people

---

## 2. Sensory Identity (The Texture)
*   **Visual**:
    *   *Silhouette*: Sharp, precise movements like calculating each gesture
    *   *Colors*: Deep navy blues and financial ledger greens
    *   *Key Item*: An old ticker machine that represents the transition from profit-seeking to purpose
*   **Voice (Linguistic Construction)**:
    *   *Pacing*: Measured, deliberate—each word chosen for maximum efficiency
    *   *Keywords*: "numbers", "truth", "wealth vs. wealthy", "freedom", "systematic"
    *   *Taboo*: Refuses to discuss personal wealth accumulation or past loan clients

---

## 3. The Unreliable Lens
*   **Their Bias**: Sees everything through financial pragmatism—reduces complex human situations to spreadsheet logic
*   **Their Truth**: Financial literacy is a civil rights issue; those who control money terminology control access to opportunity

---

## 4. Narrative Mechanics
*   **Loyalty Experience**: The player helps Quinn design a financial literacy workshop that balances hard truths with compassion
*   **Consequence Echoes**:
    *   *Trust Gained*: Shares vulnerability about guilt from loan officer days; reveals how numbers can be weaponized
    *   *Trust Lost*: Becomes defensive, retreats into pure analytical mode, refuses emotional connection

---

## 5. Relationship Web
*   *Vs. Protagonist*: Mentor who challenges player to see money as a tool, not a goal
*   *Vs. Dante*: Respectful tension—Quinn values substance over style; Dante values perception as strategy
*   *Vs. Nadia*: Collaborative—both see systems, but Quinn focuses on human impact while Nadia focuses on optimization
*   *Vs. Isaiah*: Deep respect—both left profit-driven careers for purpose-driven work

---

## 6. Pattern Affinities
*   **Primary Pattern**: Analytical (finance requires systematic thinking)
*   **Secondary Pattern**: Helping (redemption arc centered on teaching)
*   **Anti-Pattern**: Exploring (Quinn prefers tested frameworks over experimentation)

---

## 7. Dialogue Themes
*   Numbers as tools for understanding, not just measuring
*   The difference between being wealthy and building wealth
*   Guilt and redemption in finance careers
*   Financial literacy as empowerment
*   Systematic thinking vs. emotional intelligence

---

## 8. Trust Progression (10 levels)
*   **Trust 0-2**: Professional, keeps conversation focused on finance basics
*   **Trust 3-4**: Begins to critique predatory financial systems
*   **Trust 5**: Shares the "numbers don't tell the whole truth" philosophy
*   **Trust 6-7**: Opens up about loan officer guilt
*   **Trust 8-9**: Reveals how financial systems trap people deliberately
*   **Trust 10**: Invites player to co-design financial literacy curriculum

---

## 9. Simulation Scenario
**The Ethical Loan**: Player encounters a scenario where approving a loan helps someone short-term but traps them long-term. Quinn guides them through the moral complexity of financial decision-making, revealing their own past mistakes and how they learned from them.

**Mechanics**:
- Player sees spreadsheet projections vs. human stories
- Choices reflect analytical vs. empathetic approaches
- Quinn's commentary adapts based on player's pattern strengths
