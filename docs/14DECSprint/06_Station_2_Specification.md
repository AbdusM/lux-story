# Station 2: Innovation Hub - Specification

**Station Name:** Innovation Hub
**Theme:** Technology, Engineering, Design, Research
**Launch Date:** May 15, 2025 (Q2 2025)
**Price:** $9.99 DLC (or bundled with Station 1 for $14.99)
**Content:** 11 characters × 35 nodes = 385 nodes
**Target Audience:** Students exploring STEM + creative tech careers

---

## Station Concept

**Grand Central Terminus (Station 1):** A liminal space between who you were and who you're becoming. Warm, nostalgic, introspective. Characters focused on healthcare, community, philosophy, trades.

**Innovation Hub (Station 2):** A gleaming platform where the future is being built. Glass, steel, holographic displays. Characters focused on technology, engineering, design, research. Where Grand Central asks "Who are you?", Innovation Hub asks "What will you build?"

**Aesthetic:**
- **Station 1:** Warm amber lighting, vintage train schedule boards, wooden benches
- **Station 2:** Cool blue lighting, digital displays, transparent materials, prototypes scattered on tables

**Atmosphere:**
- Station 1: Contemplative, grounded, human-scale
- Station 2: Energetic, forward-looking, systems-thinking

## Character Arc Structure

All Station 2 characters follow the **Four-Part Arc Template** documented in `07_Character_Arc_Templates.md`:

### Act 1: Introduction (Nodes 1-10)
Hook, context, surface conflict, pattern introduction
- **Session Boundary:** Node 10

### Act 2: Crossroads (Nodes 11-20)
**Crossroads Scene (Nodes 11-14):**
- Key decision point
- **4 choices** (Analytical, Helping, Exploring, Building/Patience)
- No "right" answer - values revealed through choice

**Day-in-Life Pressure Test (Nodes 15-18):**
- Time-compressed urgent decisions
- Multiple simultaneous demands
- Reveals HOW player prioritizes under pressure
- Example: "3 crises, 1 hour, can handle one. Which?"

**Session Boundary:** Node 20

### Act 3: Challenge (Nodes 21-30)
**Ethical Dilemma (Nodes 21-25):**
- Professional + personal stakes
- Values conflict

**Struggle & Vulnerability (Nodes 26-30):**
- Character tries, fails, admits breaking point

**Session Boundary:** Node 30

### Act 4: Insight (Nodes 31-35)
**Unexpected Moment (Nodes 32-34):**
- Humanizing beat
- Shows why work matters emotionally
- Often relationship-based (patient, colleague, user)

**Resolution (Node 35):**
- Character decision based on journey
- Pattern crystallization
- Player acknowledgment

**Why This Structure:**
- Mobile-optimized (10 nodes = 1 session = 10-15 min)
- Pattern revelation through choices, not exposition
- Emotional arc (curious → challenged → vulnerable → transformed)
- Replayability (different choices = different understanding)

**See:** `07_Character_Arc_Templates.md` for detailed templates, examples, and AI prompts

## Risk Mitigation

### Risk 1: Station 2 Doesn't Sell (Low Player Interest)

**Likelihood:** Medium (40%)
**Impact:** Medium (revenue miss, but not fatal)

**Mitigation:**
- Price it low ($9.99) to reduce barrier
- Offer bundle discount ($14.99 for both stations)
- Run email campaign to Station 1 players: "Meet the characters building the future"
- If sales are low, make it free after 3 months (convert to marketing funnel for Stations 3-5)

---

### Risk 2: Tech Characters Don't Resonate (Players Prefer Station 1)

**Likelihood:** Low (20%)
**Impact:** Medium (need to rethink Station 3-5 strategy)

**Mitigation:**
- Ensure characters are human-first, not tech-first
- Focus on emotional arcs, not technical jargon
- Playtest with non-STEM students to verify relatability
- If tech doesn't resonate, make Station 3 healthcare (broader appeal)

---

### Risk 3: Content Creation Delays (AI Pipeline Issues)

**Likelihood:** Medium (30%)
**Impact:** High (launch delay, revenue miss)

**Mitigation:**
- Start interviews in March (1 month buffer)
- Use AI pipeline for first draft, but human review is mandatory
- Hire freelance narrative designer if needed ($2,000-5,000)
- If delayed, launch with 8 characters instead of 11 (still ~280 nodes)
