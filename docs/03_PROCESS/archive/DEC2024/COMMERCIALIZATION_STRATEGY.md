## ðŸŽ® Genre Fit Analysis

### Current Genre: Contemplative Narrative / Life Sim

**Strengths:**
- Unique contemplative gameplay (no pressure, no scores)
- Strong narrative engine with adaptive storytelling
- Character-driven (Lux the sloth)
- Already has replayability through choice branches
- Distinct visual style (Pokemon-style text boxes, Supergiant Games atmosphere)

**Market Position:**
- Fits "Simulation" category (contemplative life sim)

### Recommended Genre Positioning

**Primary:** **Simulation** (Contemplative Life Sim)
- Similar to: A Short Hike, Kind Words, Coffee Talk
- Market: Players seeking calm, reflective experiences
- Steam tags: "Simulation", "Relaxing", "Narrative", "Choices Matter"

**Secondary:** **Horror meta-genre** (if we add horror elements)
- Similar to: Doki Doki Literature Club (psychological), The Stanley Parable (meta)
- Market: Players who want thought-provoking horror without jump scares
- Steam tags: "Horror", "Psychological", "Narrative", "Atmospheric"

## âš ï¸ Risks & Mitigation

### Risk 1: Contemplative games are niche
**Mitigation:**
- Emphasize career discovery angle (broader appeal)
- Market as "life sim" not "meditation game"
- Target players who like A Short Hike, Kind Words

### Risk 2: Not enough content for $12.99
**Mitigation:**
- Ensure 5-10 hours of content minimum
- Multiple story arcs with replayability
- Hidden content for exploration
- Regular updates post-launch

### Risk 3: Marketing doesn't work
**Mitigation:**
- Start marketing early (2 months before demo)
- High volume TikTok content
- Engage with communities
- Consider paid ads if organic fails

### Risk 4: Technical issues
**Mitigation:**
- Thorough testing before demo
- Beta test with community
- Quick response to bugs
- Regular updates
