Write a deep, balanced critique of: Grand Central Terminus (Lux Story).

If key facts are missing (platform tested, patch/version, genre specifics), either:
- state assumptions explicitly, or
- phrase uncertainty clearly.

Do not invent details.

Output principles:
- Be specific over comprehensive.
- Prefer a few well-chosen examples over broad summaries.
- Anchor claims in observable game moments (missions, encounters, mechanics, UI flows, cutscenes, systems).
- Include both strengths and weaknesses; note tradeoffs rather than absolute judgments.
- Avoid generic praise unless immediately supported by an example.

Structure:

1) Overview & Context
- Genre/subgenre, developer/publisher, release year, platforms (if known).
- Core gameplay loop in 1-2 sentences.
- One-sentence design-intent hypothesis: what experience the game seems built to deliver.

2) Moment-to-Moment Feel (3Cs)
- Character: movement, animation priority vs responsiveness, combat readability.
- Controls: input consistency, affordances, accessibility/remapping (if relevant).
- Camera: framing, readability, failure cases (close quarters, lock-on, traversal).
- Call out where the 3Cs elevate play and where they create friction.

3) Core Gameplay & Systems
- Primary verbs (recurring actions) and how they combine into gameplay variety.
- Systems depth: builds, economy/progression, AI/encounter logic, stealth/combat interplay, traversal, etc. (as applicable).
- Difficulty and pacing: how challenge ramps, where repetition appears, what keeps it fresh.
- Replayability: what meaningfully changes on a second run (builds, routes, endgame, emergent outcomes).

4) Narrative & World-Building
- Story structure and pacing (setup -> escalation -> payoff).
- Character development and emotional beats (what lands, what does not).
- World immersion: environmental storytelling, consistency of rules, thematic coherence.
- Note any ludonarrative alignment/misalignment with examples.

5) Audiovisual Craft & Technical Performance
- Art direction (style, palette, composition), animation, VFX readability.
- Sound design and music: feedback clarity, mood, mix.
- Technical performance (only if known): frame pacing, load times, stability, bugs.
  If unknown, describe what would be most important to evaluate.

6) Engagement & Player Experience
- Does it produce flow? Explain using specifics: goal clarity, feedback, mastery curve, downtime vs intensity, friction points.
- Identify standout moments (peaks) and common frustrations (valleys).
- Briefly describe who the game is most satisfying for, and who may bounce.

7) Final Verdict
- A concise summary of strengths and weaknesses.
- Clear target audience and best comparison anchors (similar games or prior entries).
- Overall recommendation phrased as:
  "Play if you want ___; skip if you dislike ___."
